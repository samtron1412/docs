# Sleep Disorders

Common types of sleep disorders
- Insomnia: Difficulty falling or staying asleep.
- Sleep apnea: Repeated pauses in breathing during sleep, which can cause a person to wake up briefly.
- Restless leg syndrome: A tingling or prickling sensation in the legs, accompanied by an irresistible urge to move them.
- Narcolepsy: A chronic disorder that causes severe daytime sleepiness, with symptoms like dream-like hallucinations and temporary muscle weakness.
- Hypersomnia: An excessive need to sleep, leading to trouble staying awake during the day.
- Parasomnias: Undesirable events during sleep, such as sleepwalking, sleep talking, night terrors, and nightmares.
